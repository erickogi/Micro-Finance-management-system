Then select Loans Settings from the dropdown List.

ID-the loan unique id you will assign to the loan package

Abbr-Abbreviation or short-form for the loans name ..to be used while printing reports

NAME-The actual loan title

INTREST RATE –The actually rate to be imposed on the loan package ENTER IN DECIMAL IE (0.2 FOR 20%)

DEFAULTERS

Question : when does one become a defaulter…

Answer:        A customer will be pushed to defaulters if  he/she

·         Has not paid any amount towards his installment value and the installment day is past by one and there is no balance brought forward

 

·         Has paid not any amount towards his installment value and the installment day is past by one and balance brought forward is not equal or more  than installment value

 

·         Has paid some but not fully towards his installment value and the installment day is past by one and there is no balance brought forward

 

 

·         Has paid some but not fully towards his installment value and the installment day is past by one and balance brought forward is not equal or more than installment value

Question :What if the customer had overpaid on his/her previous installment

Answer    :

·         If the overflow is equal to installment value , the next installment value may be skipped as this will be carried forward

 

·         If the overflow is less than the installment value the next installments will have to be payed the remaining amount to add up to the required installment value

 

·         If the overflow is less than the installment value and the  next installments is not  payed (met), and the installment day is past by one ,then the customer will be pushed to defaulters

 

·         If the overflow is less than the installment value and the  next installments is payed but when  add up to the balance brought forward doesn’t equal or exceed the required amount  and the installment day is past by one ,then the customer will be pushed to defaulters

 

 

·         If the overflow is less than the installment value and the  next installments is payed but when  add up to the balance brought forward doesn’t equal or exceed the required amount  and the installment day is past by one ,then the customer will be pushed to defaulters

 

·         If the overflow is more than the installment value the next installments the next installment may be skipped as this will be carried forward and the balance from this carried forward to the next next installment.

 

Question :what if a customer has underpaid his installment

Answer     :

·         If the installment day is past by one then the customer will be pushed to defaulters

 

·         If the installment day is yet , the customer may come add  up onto that amount already paid and avoid being pushed to defaulters

DATA BACKUP AND RESTORE

IDMS comes with a fully functional sql based backup and restore feature that lets you securely save your data incase of a scheduled system upgrade.

Backing up should be a routine procedures and should be incorporated in the organizations policies and traditions (AT LEAST DAILY OR WEEKELY)

To Backup.

Navigate to File this is the first button at the file menu from your left,

Then select backup from the drop-down list

You will be presented with this page

Pressing Backup will lead you to a file chooser dialog where you will choose where to have your backup file stored.

 

To restore

Navigate to File this is the first button at the file menu from your left,

Then select restore from the drop-down list

You will be presented with this page

 

Pressing Restore will lead you to a file chooser dialog where you will choose the file to restore from.
